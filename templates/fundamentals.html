What is algorithmic complexity?

We have this notion of algorithmic complexity - a way to identify the tradeoffs of any given algorithm, both in terms of the amount of time that an algorithm will take to execute and also the amount of space that it will require in memory. For all known algorithms, both slow and fast in compute-time, we can say that for a growing input size n, the amount of time our algorithm necessitates can be articulated as a function of n itself, say, T(n). This is primarly how we can assess the speed, in terms of function behaviour, for any given algorithm.
